Bluetooth Robot Car — Introduction

What this project is — and what you'll learn

This project builds a small remote-controlled car that is driven by two DC motors and controlled wirelessly from a smartphone using a Bluetooth module (HC-05 / HC-06). The brain is an Arduino, and the motors are powered and controlled via a motor driver.

Why Bluetooth?

Bluetooth is simple to use, available on every smartphone, and perfect for low-range control. The phone sends short commands (e.g., "F" for forward, "L" for left) → the HC-05 receives them → Arduino reads and interprets → Arduino commands the motor driver to move the motors.

What you'll get from the guide

  • Full parts list and recommended modules (Arduino, HC-05, L298N/L293D)
  • Mechanical setup for a 2-motor chassis
  • Detailed wiring diagrams and photos
  • Complete Arduino code with explanation
  • Troubleshooting and enhancements (sensors, LEDs, battery tips)
